<Rimworld>
Rimworld is a colony management game.
In Rimworld, there are a few different things you need to pay attention to.
First, you need to make sure your colonists are happy. This means you need to make sure they have food, shelter, and other basic needs.
Second, you need to make sure your colonists are safe. This means you need to build defenses and make sure your colonists are well-armed.
Finally, you need to make sure your colony is growing. This means you need to build new structures, research new technologies, and recruit new colonists.
    <Map>
    The map is where your colony is located. The map is divided into tiles, each of which represents a different area of the map.
    A map is usually around 250x250 tiles.
    The map is where you will build your colony, grow crops, mine resources, and defend against enemies.
    </Map>
    <Room>
        A room is a defined area on the map that is enclosed by walls and doors. Rooms can be used for a variety of purposes, such as sleeping, eating, or working. They are good for storage, and can be used to keep your colonists happy.
        Rooms can also have different temperature levels, which can affect your colonists' mood and health.
        The maximum recommended size for a room is 11x11 tiles.
        A bedroom should be between 4x4 and 8x8 tiles.
        A dining room should be between 6x6 and 12x12 tiles.
    </Room>
    <Pawn>
    A pawn is a living creature in Rimworld. Pawns can be colonists, animals, or enemies.
    Pawns have a variety of needs, including food, rest, and recreation. Pawns also have skills, which determine how good they are at different tasks.
    Each pawn only takes up one tile on the map, so you can have a lot of pawns on the map at once.
    </Pawn>
    <Colonist>
    A colonist is a pawn that is part of your colony. Colonists can be assigned to different tasks, such as cooking, cleaning, or researching.
    Colonists can also be equipped with weapons and armor to defend the colony from enemies.
    Colonists can also form relationships with each other, which can have both positive and negative effects on the colony.
    </Colonist>
    <Animal>
    An animal is a pawn that is not part of your colony. Animals can be tamed and trained to perform different tasks, such as hauling items or attacking enemies.
    Animals can also be used for food, clothing, and other resources.
    Some animals are hostile and will attack your colonists, so it's important to be careful when dealing with them.
    </Animal>
    <Enemy>
    An enemy is a pawn that is hostile to your colony. Enemies can be human, animal, or mechanoid.
    Enemies will attack your colony and try to destroy your buildings and kill your colonists.
    You can defend against enemies by building defenses, equipping your colonists with weapons, and training animals to attack enemies.
    </Enemy>
    <Building>
    A building is a structure that you can construct in Rimworld. Buildings can be used for a variety of purposes, such as housing, storage, production, and defense.
    Buildings can be made out of different materials, such as wood or steel.
    You can also deconstruct buildings to reclaim the materials used to build them.
    </Building>
    <Item>
    An item is an object that can be used by your colonists. Items can be weapons, armor, food, or resources.
    Items can be found on the map, traded with other factions, or crafted by your colonists.
    Items can also deteriorate over time, so it's important to keep them in good condition.
    </Item>
    <Food>
    Food is a resource that your colonists need to survive. Colonists can eat raw food, but they will get more nutrition from cooked food.
    Food can be grown on farms, hunted from animals, or traded with other factions.
    Food can also spoil over time, so it's important to store it in a refrigerated area.
    </Food>
    <Research>
    Research is a mechanic in Rimworld that allows you to unlock new technologies and buildings. Research is done at a research bench by a colonist with the research skill.
    Researching new technologies can give your colony a significant advantage, such as better weapons, armor, or defenses.
    Researching new technologies also requires resources, so it's important to manage your resources carefully.
    </Research>
